GCU D1 Men's Volleyball Program Termination

GCU Men's Volleyball Program History

Where/When It Began

The GCU Men’s Volleyball program was established in 2008 as part of Grand Canyon University’s push to elevate its athletics to a national level. From humble beginnings, the team quickly grew into a respected competitor in NCAA Division I.

Awards and Accomplishments

Over the years, GCU Men’s Volleyball built a strong reputation, earning national rankings, competing in the tough MPSF (Mountain Pacific Sports Federation), and producing numerous All-American players. The program consistently made postseason appearances and was recognized for its athleticism, sportsmanship, and competitive spirit.

Impact

More than just wins and stats, the program served as a foundation for student-athlete development. It offered scholarships, built lifelong bonds, and brought national visibility to the university. The team helped foster a sense of community on campus, drawing loyal crowds and representing GCU on a national stage.

Elimination of the Program

Reasoning

On May 9, 2024, Grand Canyon University announced its decision to cut the Men’s Volleyball program, citing a strategic reallocation of resources and Title IX compliance as primary reasons. While the university stated this was a difficult but necessary move, the lack of transparency left many with more questions than answers. Grand Canyon University (GCU) cut its men's volleyball program in April 2025 to "better support" their other 20+ athletic programs, according to the university. This decision was made despite the team's success. The university also mentioned the evolving college athletics landscape. Their plans to join the Mountain West Conference played a role in the cut as they don't sponsor men's volleyball.

Unexpectedness/ Players Blindsided

Players, coaches, and supporters were blindsided by the announcement. There was no public discussion, no warning signs. Athletes had been preparing for the upcoming season, only to learn suddenly that their sport—and in many cases, their scholarships and futures—were no longer part of GCU’s plans.

Players

The decision directly impacted dozens of student-athletes and staff, many of whom had relocated across the country to be part of the program. Some are now scrambling to find new schools or give up volleyball altogether. For seniors, the chance to finish their careers as Lopes was taken away without notice.

Aftermath

Reactions

The reaction has been swift and emotional. Players, alumni, families, and fans have expressed deep disappointment. Social media has been flooded with posts from current and former athletes sharing memories, voicing frustration, and demanding answers.

What Are People Doing to Try to Keep It

Petitions have been launched. Letters are being written. Alumni, parents, and supporters are organizing fundraising efforts and contacting university officials to reconsider the decision. Former players and coaches are speaking out, advocating for the program’s reinstatement and highlighting its importance.

Call to Action

If you believe in the value of collegiate athletics, the right of student-athletes to compete, and the importance of honoring commitments, speak up. Share your story. Sign the petition. Write to GCU leadership. This program was more than just a team. It was a family—and it deserves a second chance.
